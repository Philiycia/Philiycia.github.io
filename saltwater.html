Clownfish

Beginner

Scientific Name: Amphiprion ocellaris

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons (75 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-28¬∞C (75-82¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.0-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 6-10 years
  • Diet: Pellets, flakes, live food
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Care Tips

Clownfish are omnivorous and will eat a variety of foods including high-quality marine pellets, frozen mysis shrimp, and brine shrimp. Feed twice daily with small portions. In the wild, they form a symbiotic relationship with sea anemones, receiving protection in exchange for feeding the anemone.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Sex Change Ability: All clownfish are born male. The largest becomes female, and if she dies, the dominant male changes sex.
  • Finding Nemo Fame: The character Dory popularized clownfish, leading to increased captive-breeding to protect wild reefs.
  • Immunity to Stings: They have special mucus coating protecting them from anemone tentacles through gradual acclimation.

Firefish

Intermediate

Scientific Name: Nemateleotris magnifica

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 30 gallons (113 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-27¬∞C (75-81¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.1-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 4-8 years
  • Diet: Mysis shrimp, copepods, pellets
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Care Tips

Firefish are carnivorous and prefer small meaty foods. Feed high-quality frozen or live mysis shrimp, copepods, and brine shrimp 2-3 times daily. Provide sandy substrate where they can dig and create shelter. They're extremely fast swimmers, so the tank should have plenty of hiding spots.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Rapid Movement: Can accelerate from 0-60 mph in fractions of a second, making them some of the fastest fish.
  • Symbiotic Burrow Sharing: Share burrows with pistol shrimp in mutual benefit relationships in the wild.
  • Goby-Like Behavior: They readily accept captive-bred surroundings and create territory in tanks like gobies.

Blue Tang

Intermediate

Scientific Name: Paracanthurus hepatus

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 75 gallons (283 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-26¬∞C (75-79¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.1-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years
  • Diet: Algae, pellets, live food
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Care Tips

Blue Tangs are primarily herbivorous and require plenty of algae and vegetable matter. Feed dried seaweed, algae pellets, and spirulina supplemented with small meaty foods. They graze throughout the day, so frequent small feedings work better. A well-established tank with natural algae growth is essential.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Toxic Defense: Blue Tangs have venomous spines near their tail that cause painful injuries to predators and humans.
  • Movie Fame: The character Dory from Finding Nemo/Finding Dory sparked massive demand for Blue Tangs.
  • Algae Grazer Specialist: A single Blue Tang can consume over 3 pounds of algae per year in the wild.

Lionfish

Advanced

Scientific Name: Pterois

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 50 gallons (189 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-26¬∞C (75-79¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.1-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Diet: Live or frozen fish
  • Difficulty: Advanced

Care Tips

Lionfish are strictly carnivorous predators requiring live or recently frozen fish. Feed feeder fish or silversides 2-3 times weekly. Handle with extreme care during tank maintenance - wear thick gloves. They're ambush predators that hunt actively in the evening. Never stick hands directly into the tank.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Venomous Predator: One of the few venomous fish in the ocean - venom is purely defensive, not for hunting.
  • Invasive Species: Native to Indo-Pacific but became invasive in the Atlantic with no natural predators.
  • Elegant Hunting: Use elaborate fins to herd and trap prey fish, then swallow them whole in a fraction of a second.

Mandarin Fish

Advanced

Scientific Name: Synchiropus splendidus

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons (75 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-26¬∞C (75-79¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.1-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 2-5 years
  • Diet: Tiny live foods only
  • Difficulty: Advanced

Care Tips

Mandarin fish are extremely challenging as they only eat tiny live foods including copepods and amphipods. They will not accept prepared foods. A well-established tank with abundant natural zooplankton is essential. Many fail because they starve in clean aquariums without sufficient live food populations.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Most Colorful Fish: One of the most stunningly colored fish in the ocean with intricate electric blue, orange, and green patterns.
  • Synchronized Mating Dance: Perform elaborate mating dances at dusk, synchronizing movements and rising together to spawn.
  • Hardest to Keep: Have a reputation for being the hardest fish to keep alive due to strict dietary requirements.

Seahorse

Advanced

Scientific Name: Hippocampus

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 40 gallons (150 liters) for pair
  • Water Temperature: 23-26¬∞C (73-79¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 8.1-8.3
  • Salinity: 1.020-1.025
  • Lifespan: 4-6 years
  • Diet: Live mysis shrimp, copepods
  • Difficulty: Advanced

Care Tips

Seahorses are extremely slow feeders requiring live food. They primarily eat tiny copepods and live mysis shrimp, and must be hand-fed. Require calm, low-flow tanks with plenty of seagrass or gorgonians for anchoring. They cannot compete with aggressive fish and will starve if placed with faster-feeding species.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Male Pregnancy: Seahorses are the only fish where males become pregnant, incubating eggs in a pouch.
  • Monogamous Partners: Highly unusual for being monogamous, staying together and recognizing each other individually.
  • Conservation Concern: Threatened by habitat loss and illegal collection - most aquarium specimens are now sustainably farmed.
