About Fish and Aquarium Care

Understanding aquatic life and proper fish care

What Are Fish?

Fish are aquatic vertebrate animals that are cold-blooded, have gills for breathing underwater, fins for movement, and scales for protection. They are found in virtually every aquatic environment on Earth, from freshwater streams to the deepest ocean trenches.

Malaysia's position in the tropical region means it hosts incredible biodiversity of both freshwater and saltwater fish species. These fish have adapted to their specific environments over millions of years, making them unique and fascinating subjects for aquarium keeping.

Freshwater Fish vs Saltwater Fish

Key Differences

Water Salinity

Freshwater: Less than 0.5 ppt (parts per thousand)

Saltwater: 30-40 ppt with dissolved salts and minerals

Fish Osmoregulation

Freshwater Fish: Their bodies are more concentrated than surrounding water, so they absorb water passively and must actively excrete excess water through dilute urine.

Saltwater Fish: Their bodies are less concentrated than surrounding water, so they lose water passively and must drink seawater to compensate, excreting excess salt through gills.

Tank Maintenance

Freshwater: Generally easier to maintain, more forgiving water conditions, suitable for beginners

Saltwater: More complex chemistry, requires precise salinity levels, requires more advanced aquarium knowledge

Cost Considerations

Freshwater: Lower equipment and fish costs, less maintenance equipment needed

Saltwater: Higher initial investment, specialized equipment, more expensive livestock
