Betta Fish

Beginner

Scientific Name: Betta splendens

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 5 gallons (20 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-27¬∞C (75-81¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.5-7.5
  • Lifespan: 3-5 years
  • Diet: High-protein pellets, bloodworms
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Care Tips

Bettas are carnivorous and require high-protein foods. Feed premium betta pellets once or twice daily. Supplement with live foods like bloodworms 2-3 times weekly. While bettas can survive in small containers, they thrive in larger tanks with proper filtration and plants.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Labyrinth Organ: Bettas can breathe air directly from the surface, allowing survival in low-oxygen environments.
  • Fighting Fish: "Betta" means "warrior" - they were bred for fighting in ancient Siamese traditions.
  • Color Changers: Bettas change color based on mood, age, and diet, displaying vibrant colors when healthy.

Guppy

Beginner

Scientific Name: Poecilia reticulata

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 10 gallons (38 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 22-28¬∞C (72-82¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.8-7.8
  • Lifespan: 2-3 years
  • Diet: Flake food, pellets, small live foods
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Care Tips

Guppies are omnivorous and thrive on quality flake food supplemented with small live foods. Feed small portions twice daily. They're excellent community fish and will nibble algae and plant matter. Variety in diet promotes vibrant coloration and overall health.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Rainbow Variations: Male guppies display incredible color variety - no two males are exactly alike.
  • Live Bearers: Females can store sperm and produce multiple batches of fry without additional male contact.
  • Adaptability: Guppies are extremely adaptable and have been introduced worldwide for mosquito control.

Goldfish

Beginner

Scientific Name: Carassius auratus

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons (75 liters) for first fish
  • Water Temperature: 18-24¬∞C (64-75¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.5-8.5
  • Lifespan: 10-20 years
  • Diet: Pellets, vegetables, live food
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Care Tips

Goldfish are omnivorous and require a balanced diet of high-quality pellets supplemented with vegetables like blanched peas. Feed twice daily, only what they can consume in 5 minutes. They produce significant waste and require robust filtration and frequent tank maintenance.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Long Memory: Goldfish have excellent memory and can recognize their owner, contrary to popular myth.
  • Ancient Heritage: Selectively bred in China over 1,500 years ago from wild carp.
  • Waste Producers: Produce roughly 2.5 times more waste than tropical fish of similar size.

Angelfish

Intermediate

Scientific Name: Pterophyllum

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons (75 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 24-28¬∞C (75-82¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.0-6.5
  • Lifespan: 8-10 years
  • Diet: Quality pellets, live food, small insects
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Care Tips

Angelfish are carnivorous and prefer high-protein diets. Feed quality pellets twice daily, supplemented with live foods like bloodworms and brine shrimp. Angelfish will eat small fish, so tank mates should be appropriately sized. Vary their diet regularly for vibrant coloration.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Devoted Parents: Mated pairs guard eggs and fry fiercely, with both parents participating in care.
  • Color Morphs: Through selective breeding, they come in marble, koi, gold, platinum, and striped varieties.
  • Body Shape: Their triangular shape provides incredible maneuverability in dense vegetation.

Neon Tetra

Beginner

Scientific Name: Paracheirodon innesi

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 10 gallons (38 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 20-24¬∞C (68-75¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.0-7.0
  • Lifespan: 5-8 years
  • Diet: Small pellets, flake food, micro live food
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Care Tips

Neon Tetras have small mouths and require small food particles. Feed quality micro pellets and finely crushed flake food twice daily. Supplement with tiny live foods like infusoria and copepods. Keep in groups of at least 6-8 for optimal color display and natural behavior.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Bioluminescent Colors: Their electric blue stripe is created by light refraction, not actual light production.
  • Sleep Behavior: They lose their vibrant stripe at night and change color for camouflage while sleeping.
  • Migration: In nature, they form massive schools of thousands that migrate seasonally through river systems.

Discus Fish

Advanced

Scientific Name: Symphysodon

Basic Care Information

  • Tank Size: Minimum 40 gallons (150 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 26-30¬∞C (79-86¬∞F)
  • pH Level: 6.0-6.5
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Diet: High-quality pellets, live food essential
  • Difficulty: Advanced

Care Tips

Discus are carnivorous with demanding nutritional requirements. They require premium quality pellets and live foods like bloodworms should constitute 30-40% of their diet. Feed small portions 3-4 times daily as discus have small stomachs. They require pristine water quality with frequent water changes.

üí° Fun Facts

  • Parental Mucus Feeding: Parents produce nutritious mucus on their skin that fry eat by nibbling for weeks.
  • Selective Breeding: Available in red, blue, yellow, turquoise, and solid color patterns - premium morphs cost hundreds.
  • Sound Sensitivity: Extremely sensitive to vibrations and loud noises, requiring a quiet environment.
